# Blog 2 Structural Review: "We Could Have Had AGI By Now"

**Type:** Story anatomy — narrative arcs, idea progression, structural form
**Date:** 2026-03-13

---

## 1. CORE MESSAGE

**The building blocks for durable, self-improving agents have existed for months — what was missing was not a smarter model but the bet to scale architecture (complex system design) instead of scaling the model.**

The essay reframes AGI as an organism that emerges from simple primitives interacting, not an engineered artifact that gets bigger.

---

## 2. STORY BLOCKS (6 Narrative Phases)

### Phase 1: THE PROVOCATION (Opening → "scale the architecture, not just the model")
- **Sections:** Opening 4 paragraphs (no heading)
- **Accomplishes:** Drops the thesis cold. Defines "the practical kind" of AGI — persistent, self-improving, $100/month. Introduces the complex system vs. engineered artifact distinction. Makes the bet explicit.
- **Ends at:** "scale the architecture, not just the model."

### Phase 2: THE REFRAME (LLMs Are Electricity → A Lawyer Is Not One Giant Thought)
- **Sections:** "LLMs Are Electricity. Agents Are Toasters. (Again.)", "A Lawyer Is Not One Giant Thought"
- **Accomplishes:** Calls back to Blog 1 (electricity/toaster), then immediately pivots: don't build a toaster either — build a complex system. Introduces the "organization, not intelligence" thesis. Generation problem solved; preservation problem remains.
- **Key move:** "do not build a toaster" — reverses Blog 1's ending instruction, escalating to a higher design principle.
- **Ends at:** "Even with a smaller model as the engine."

### Phase 3: THE BIOLOGY (Where Does the Context Come From → Biology Did Not Scale One Molecule)
- **Sections:** "Where Does the Context Come From?", "Biology Did Not Scale One Molecule"
- **Accomplishes:** The essay's intellectual core. Context composition diagnostic. mRNA vs. differentiated cell. Cortical columns from *A Thousand Brains*. Giant mRNA blockquote ("When we scale only the model, we are building the giant mRNA"). Complex systems toolkit (feedback loops, emergence, mental models). Compartmentalization callback to Blog 1. Introduces Ralph Loop as a primitive.
- **Note:** Longest phase (~40% of essay). Dense with ideas. Two H2 sections that could almost be separate phases, but they share the same project: proving biology is "the category, not the analogy."
- **Ends at:** "You are no longer just blocking a stop signal. You are building a job system."

### Phase 4: THE MECHANISM (The Heartbeat → Hooks Are the Missing Evolutionary Layer → Consolidation)
- **Sections:** "The Heartbeat: Where Agency Actually Begins", "Hooks Are the Missing Evolutionary Layer", "Consolidation: Where the System Learns"
- **Accomplishes:** Translates biology into architecture. Heartbeat = job system with stop-blocking. Hooks = sensory layer. Consolidation = how the system learns. Each section names a concrete primitive.
- **Ends at:** "This is how you get professional competence without requiring the main model to internalize everything."

### Phase 5: THE VISION (The Seed Agent → Internalization → What a Year Looks Like → The Practical Roadmap)
- **Sections:** "The Seed Agent: Small Structure That Grows", "Internalization Is Not the Answer", "What a Year of Experience Looks Like", "The Practical Roadmap"
- **Accomplishes:** Seeds planted in Phase 3 now bloom. Seed agent defined (4 capabilities). Internalization objection dispatched. "Two copies" thought experiment makes the compound advantage visceral. Roadmap crystallizes everything into 4 numbered layers.
- **Ends at:** "Like a cell that does not just process nutrients but also repairs itself, manages waste, and maintains its own membrane."

### Phase 6: THE LANDING (Why I Say "We Could Have Had It by Now" → The Question Was Always Wrong)
- **Sections:** "Why I Say 'We Could Have Had It by Now'", "The Question Was Always Wrong"
- **Accomplishes:** Returns to title. Names AutoGPT/BabyAGI as failed engineered systems. Reframes the industry's question. Closes on "Build the organism."
- **Ends at:** "Build the organism."

## 4. FRAGMENTATION CHECK

### Tight (no action needed):
- **Organism/biology arc** — well-threaded across all phases, 14 appearances
- **Complex vs. engineered** — clean binary, runs full length
- **Scale architecture thesis** — transforms rather than repeats
- **Blog 1 callbacks** — specific and well-placed

### Potentially fragmented:

**Phase 3 is doing 5 jobs at once:**
1. Context composition diagnostic ("Where Does the Context Come From?")
2. mRNA vs. differentiated cell analogy
3. Cortical columns / *A Thousand Brains*
4. Complex systems toolkit (feedback loops, emergence, mental models, Parrish reference)
5. Compartmentalization callback + Ralph Loop + job system introduction

These are all in service of "biology is the category," but the section runs ~40% of the essay. The reader absorbs the mRNA analogy, then cortical columns, then a Shane Parrish book recommendation, then compartmentalization, then Ralph Loop — five ideas in rapid succession. The section never pauses to let the reader consolidate.

**"Ralph Loop" appears once then is superseded:**
- Introduced in Phase 3: "Ralph Loop is a plugin that does exactly this"
- Immediately replaced in Phase 4 by "the job system" which does more
- Functions as a stepping stone, but a reader encountering it for the first time may not realize it's being replaced within two paragraphs

**"Control knobs" metaphor appears once, never returns:**
- Phase 3: "These are the control knobs of agent architecture"
- The slide-deck analogy ("the way you once learned that a presentation is made of slides") is vivid but also one-off
- Neither metaphor recurs — they do local work but don't contribute to any arc

**"Primitives" — stated multiple times but only partially demonstrated:**
1. Phase 3: "if intelligence is a complex system, what are the primitives?"
2. Phase 3: "files, directories, instruction files"
3. Phase 4: heartbeat, hooks, consolidation
4. Phase 5: "persistent state, interception hooks, compartmentalized work, and consolidation mechanism"
5. Phase 6: "persistence, hooks, jobs, consolidation"

The word "primitives" appears 5 times, with the list varying slightly each time (3 items, then demonstrated individually, then 4 items, then 4 slightly different items). The final enumeration in Phase 6 does not perfectly match the seed agent's 4 capabilities listed in Phase 5. Minor inconsistency, but a careful reader might notice the lists don't align.

## 5. ANATOMICAL FORM

**Shape: FUNNEL → BLUEPRINT**

The essay begins wide (AGI, biology, evolution, complex systems) and narrows relentlessly toward specific architectural primitives and a numbered roadmap.

```
WIDE    ← AGI thesis, complex system definition
 \      ← Toaster reversal, lawyer = pipeline
  \     ← Context diagnostic, mRNA vs. cell, cortical columns
   \    ← Complex systems toolkit, compartmentalization, Ralph Loop
    \   ← Heartbeat (job system), hooks (sensory layer)
     \  ← Consolidation, seed agent, internalization rebuttal
      | ← Two copies thought experiment
      | ← 4-layer roadmap (numbered, concrete)
      ▼ ← "Build the organism."
```

**Does the shape serve the core message?** Yes. The funnel earns the roadmap. By the time the reader reaches the 4 numbered layers, every concept has already been introduced through metaphor and analogy. The essay does the "show, then formalize" move well.

**One structural risk:** The funnel is steep in Phase 3 — the biology section introduces so many ideas that the narrowing feels rushed. The funnel would be smoother if Phase 3 shed one idea (the Shane Parrish reference or the extended cortical columns passage) or split the biology section with a breathing moment.

## 7. ADJACENT IDEAS

### Well-positioned:
- **mRNA vs. differentiated cell** — the essay's best extended analogy. Earns the blockquote. Makes "scale architecture not model" visceral in a way pure argument cannot.
- **Cortical columns / *A Thousand Brains*** — strong parallel between cortical column and LLM. Bridges neuroscience and AI without overreaching.
- **"Two copies" thought experiment** (Phase 5) — the essay's most powerful proof. Immediately persuasive. Correctly placed after all concepts are introduced.
- **Internalization rebuttal** (Phase 5) — tight, two-point argument. Doesn't overstay. Dispatches the main objection efficiently.
- **"A lawyer is not one giant thought"** — memorable section heading. Sets up the organization-vs-intelligence thesis that runs through the whole essay.

### Watch for:
- **Shane Parrish / *The Great Mental Models* reference** — useful but creates a register shift mid-Phase 3. The essay moves from visceral biology ("mRNA," "cortical columns") to a book recommendation. The reader pauses to process a new name and title. Consider whether this could be a footnote or end-of-essay resource rather than inline.
- **Ralph Loop → job system transition** — Ralph Loop gets a full paragraph including a GitHub link, then is immediately superseded by the job system. A reader may wonder why Ralph Loop was introduced at all. The stepping-stone logic is valid ("start here, then extend"), but the transition is abrupt.
- **"Control knobs" + slide-deck analogy** — two adjacent metaphors that do local work but don't recur. The slide-deck comparison (presentations have slides, agents have building blocks) is accessible but reads slightly tutorial-like in an essay that otherwise runs at philosophical altitude.
- **Joint embedding models / diffusion-based LLMs** (Phase 3) — names two emerging technologies with inline links. Correctly supports the "agent metabolizes any engine" point, but adds processing load (two technical names + two links in one sentence). The point could land with one example instead of two.
- **AutoGPT / BabyAGI** (Phase 6) — named as failed engineered systems. Brief and correctly placed. No issue, but worth noting: these names will date the essay. They serve the argument well now.
