Welcome. This is an audio guide to the interactive deck called From Apprentice to Architect. Let me walk you through what it shows and how to explore it.

First, how to move around. Use the left and right arrow keys to walk the seven steps in order. That is the main story, told from beginning to end. The up and down arrow keys open a deeper detail card about the step you are on. You can click any box on the screen to read three things about it: what it is, why it matters, and what happens under the hood. There is a help button in the corner if you ever want these instructions again.

Now, the big idea. A seed agent is not frozen when you first run it. It carries five commitments from the very first session, it guards every change to its own machinery behind a single gate, and it grows leaner and more precise over time. This deck walks that whole story.

Step one is who the seed is. Before any job runs, the seed reasons from five identity facts it carries everywhere. It knows it is extendable, not a fixed product. It knows that edits to its own plugin layer are privileged and require a deliberate unlock. It knows those unlocks must come from the user, never from the seed itself. It knows the design blogs are a source of self-knowledge it can consult. And it knows the usual three-role triangle of agent, developer, and user collapses to two: a non-developer operator becomes the architect of their own seed.

Step two is the customization guardrail. One OR-gate controls every plugin-code edit. Two arms can each open the lock independently. The first arm is gmode: the operator enters a deliberate maintenance lane with a written justification. The second arm is a user-approved job: the seed proposed, the user confirmed, and the focused job carries an approval flag. If neither arm is open, the gate blocks by default. Admission is an OR; the block needs both arms to fail.

Step three is propose, then confirm. The seed cannot self-authorize plugin work. Any working phase can notice a need and drop a note in its footer. But only CONDENSE, the synthesis phase, may turn that note into a formal proposal. The user judges: approve, redirect, or reject. On approval, a hook flips the job's approval flag. The agent then focuses the approved job, and inside it the second arm of the guardrail is open.

Step four is the five customization layers. Think of an altitude ladder from deepest to shallowest. At the bottom is the phasic substrate itself: the cycle shape and transition maps. Above that are hard controls, the phase guards that block with a code-level refusal. Then soft voices: coaching the agent may follow. Then per-job yaml, which lets one job tailor the coaching for its own duration. And at the top, per-job plugins: aspirational today, the far end of what customization could reach. The rule is simple: pick the shallowest layer that solves the problem. A misfiring nudge is a voice edit, not a code change.

Step five is how the brain changes shape. A young seed has a large brain and few hooks. A mature seed has a lean brain and many hooks. The canonical flow moves proven patterns from voice to hook, then removes them from the brain's prose. Size limits force this: when the brain fills, the oldest proven patterns are the best candidates for extraction into code. The brain gets leaner, not bigger.

Step six is the cost ladder for a single behavioral concern. Every concern starts as a voice: soft, ignorable coaching. If measurement shows the voice failing to hold, it climbs to a hook inside an existing plugin. If it needs its own state, it earns a whole new plugin. And if a pattern becomes so universal that every plugin should inherit it automatically, it fossilizes into the plugin template. Each climb is gated by the over-engineering veto: measurement first, then friction.

Step seven is the operator's parallel arc. While the seed matures, the operator walks from Apprentice to Journeyman to Architect. An apprentice supervises: learning when to advance, when to bail, how to declare depth honestly. A journeyman directs: cycles are smoother, and work settles into repeatable shapes. An architect composes: most cycles complete without intervention on the basics, and new-plugin creation is routine. Nothing gates the arc. An operator can stay an apprentice, or move faster. The seed makes the patient path easier, not mandatory.

Take it one card at a time. Each is a single focused idea. Press the right arrow to begin at the five facts.
