Welcome. This is an audio guide to the interactive diagram called Inside a Phase — the three-family exit gate.

Let me first tell you how to move around. Press the left and right arrow keys to walk through six steps in order. That sequence is the main story. Press down to open a deeper detail card about whichever step you are on. Press up to come back. And you can click any box on the screen to read three things about it: what it is, why it matters, and what happens under the hood in the real code. Hover any arrow between boxes to see what that connection means. The question-mark button in the corner brings these instructions back any time.

Now for the story this deck tells. Every phase of the seed agent's phasic brain follows the same shape. The entry is completely frictionless — there is no gate to pass, no value to set, nothing required before you begin. The entry voice simply coaches you: here is how to do this kind of work, here are the subagents to dispatch. Quietly, in the background, the phase rolls its own difficulty for later — drawing at random how many reflective lenses it will demand, and how many new marked notes. You do not see those numbers yet. They wait at the exit.

Step two is doing the actual work — gathering, deciding, building, checking, or consolidating, depending on the phase. While the work happens, a second rhythm called the min-max gate paces it from inside. The min side says: before you write down a conclusion, you must have done enough investigation. The max side says: once any activity class hits its ceiling, you must stop and write before gathering more. One clean event — writing into working memory, a CLAUDE.md update — resets every class counter at once. This gate paces the work. It is entirely separate from the gate that decides whether you can leave.

Step three is the moment you try to leave. You run the force-advance commit, the only forward door out of a phase. If you have not yet reflected enough, the exit voice fires. Same phase, but now a different voice: stop doing, start examining your own cognition. Here is what is missing. Fill it, then try again.

Step four is the advance condition itself: the three-family exit gate. Three distinct kinds of evidence that the phase actually reflected on its own work. Family A asks whether the in-session reflection commands ran — two mandatory core operations that run every phase no matter what, plus a drawn number of deeper mental-model lenses. Family B asks whether the phase left behind genuinely new marked notes in working memory. This check is soft by default, coaching without blocking. Family C asks whether at least one of the phase's available reflector subagents ran — a file-only reviewer tuned to that phase's specific failure modes. OBSERVE has a blindspot-finder. PLAN has a premortem. EXECUTE has a drift-auditor. VERIFY has a meta-audit. CONDENSE has a promotion-scan.

Step five shows both outcomes. Satisfy the required families and the phase can advance — but only on top of the other commit requirements: the shape sections must be present, the working-memory update floor must be met, the message must be long enough. The three-family gate adds to those requirements; it never replaces them. Miss a hard family and the advance is refused. The phase stays put, the gap is named, and you satisfy it and retry. Backward recovery, moving to an earlier phase, has no cognitive gates at all — only a clean git tree. Recovery is always available, always free.

Step six is CONDENSE, which bends the gate slightly. It keeps families A and C but is not gated on family B, because its whole job is to consume marked notes, not produce them. Instead, it adds its own eighty-percent deflation gate — the cycle's working-memory footers must shrink by at least eighty percent before CONDENSE can advance. And its marker-side check runs in reverse: for each class of marked note, it must dispatch a dedicated consuming subagent, and drive every note to a terminal state.

That is the full shape. Entry is coached and free. Work is paced by the min-max gate from inside. Exit demands three kinds of evidence of reflection. Take it one step at a time. Press the right arrow to begin.
