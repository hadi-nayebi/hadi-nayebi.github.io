How to read this deck

The phasic brain.

The seed agent does not free-style. Any serious work — drafting a contract, debugging a system, planning a migration — actually asks for several different cognitive states: gathering information is a different mode of thought from deciding an approach, which is different again from building, from checking, from banking what you learned. Mix them and the work suffers — you build before you understand, you “verify” work you are still attached to, you never write down the lesson. The seed’s answer is a Markov phasic brain: a focused job moves through a fixed cycle of cognition compartments — idle, then OBSERVE, PLAN, EXECUTE, VERIFY, CONDENSE, and back to idle — and each phase may make only ONE kind of move. The tools for the other modes are physically withheld: OBSERVE cannot write code, EXECUTE cannot keep re-planning. Forbidding the other moves IS the discipline — it holds the agent in the right cognitive state at the right time and stops the states from bleeding together. The order is not red tape; it is the natural shape of effective work made enforceable: understand before you decide, decide before you build, build before you check, check before you bank the lesson. This deck walks the whole skeleton — the cycle itself, the two maps that define every legal transition, the conductor and its five self-guarding musicians, the altered list, anchor write authority, the gmode maintenance lane, and the fractal Markov-brain framing underneath it all. Each card is one focused idea, with a diagram you can hover and click.
