# Structured Questions — `question_discipline`

*Essay 5.6 — The Always-On Digital Cortex, Part 6 of 9.*

---

[Essay 5.5](05_5-interaction-summary.html) covered the mega-prompt that grows with every question. This part covers the gate that grew the *asking surface* itself into something architectural — every question the seed agent asks the user carries a registered prefix, and the rest of the seed's machinery dispatches on those prefixes.

---

## What it owns

`question_discipline` exists to make every question the seed agent asks the user *structured*. It works by gating every `AskUserQuestion` call against a small list of registered prefixes — only registered question forms get through. Most registered forms are ceremonies (locks, approvals, completion claims), and `[WAITING]` is the deliberate generic escape valve. It applies on the main agent's `AskUserQuestion` surface, with one deliberate runtime exception for dispatched subagents. *[ref: question-discipline-exists-to-make | .claude/plugins/question_discipline/hooks/question-discipline-gate.sh:47 + :67-78 (readonly PREFIX_REGISTRY) + .claude/context/job-system.md "Question shape" | Subagent bypass at line 47: `[[ "${CLAUDE_SUBAGENT:-}" == "true" ]] && exit 0`. The live `PREFIX_REGISTRY` holds the registered prefixes: the shaped ceremonies `[PLUGIN-LOCK]`, `[GMODE]`, `[JOB-COMPLETE]`, and the paired plugin-edit approvals `[JOB-APPROVE-CREATION]` (creation-time) + `[JOB-APPROVE-PLUGIN]` (mid-life) — plus the lighter `[TEST-LOCK]`, `[REPORT-TO-UPSTREAM]`, `[REPEAT-JOB]`, and the generic `[WAITING]`. Earlier drafts of the prototype carried separate prefixes for approving a plan; that decision now rides entirely on `[JOB-COMPLETE]` at the final cycle's CONDENSE, and the registry has consolidated around the shaped ceremonies above.]*

## How it works — batch-cascade against the registry

A pre-call hook walks every question in the call and matches its first token against a small registry of approved prefixes hardcoded in the script. The registry is short — most entries name very specific ceremonies (unlocking a plugin, approving a job, promoting a job to repeating, reporting upstream, authorizing a plugin-editing job at creation, and the others), and one (`[WAITING]`) is the deliberate generic — a catch-all for the cases where the seed agent legitimately needs input from the user and the situation does not fit one of the structured ceremonies. The prefixes cascade: when the agent batches multiple questions in one call, *every* question must carry a registered prefix, or the entire batch is rejected and the agent has to rewrite. The plugin owns no hidden state; the registry is its only state. *[ref: pre-call-hook-walks-questions | .claude/plugins/question_discipline/hooks/question-discipline-gate.sh:101-135 | Batch-cascade: comment "defense against multi-question batches sneaking unprefixed past. Validate ALL, not just [0]." `for ((i = 0; i < QCOUNT; i++))` iterates every question, sets `all_ok=0` on any failure; line 135 `if [[ "$all_ok" -eq 0 ]]` rejects entire batch.]*

Dispatched subagents are flagged as such and bypass the gate because they are not supposed to become a second user-facing conversation surface. Their job is focused investigation; if they need user input, the cleaner pattern is to report the uncertainty back to the parent agent, which asks through the registered prefix system. The bypass is the one runtime exception the registry admits — every main-agent ask must clear a prefix, no matter which plugin's hook is firing or which phase the agent is in. This draws the boundary at the user-facing main-agent surface; extending the same discipline recursively into subagents is a customization choice, not the current prototype's default. *[ref: subagent-bypass-runtime-exception | .claude/plugins/question_discipline/hooks/question-discipline-gate.sh:46-47 | The bypass is one line: `[[ "${CLAUDE_SUBAGENT:-}" == "true" ]] && exit 0`. Comment above it cites `feedback_subagent_guard_scope` — "subagents bypass behavioral gates but not structural ones. Question discipline is behavioral." Subagents inherit a CLAUDE_SUBAGENT env flag from the Agent tool wrapper; the gate exits 0 before reading INPUT or walking PREFIX_REGISTRY.]*

## How shape compels cognition — per-prefix admission gates

Past the registry gate, every admitted prefix lands in its owner plugin's per-prefix handler — and that handler is where the shape lives. Each handler demands a different slot-set the seed agent must fill before the question reaches the user. `[JOB-COMPLETE]` checks several slots: the focused job's name in the body, the literal `Review` option label, the literal `Approve completion` option label, and a ≥100-word body. `[JOB-APPROVE-CREATION]` — the ceremony that authorizes a plugin-touching job (Essay 5.9) — demands its own slot-set naming the proposed objective, the plugins to touch, why now, and the risk surface. `[PLUGIN-LOCK]` and `[TEST-LOCK]` check a ≥100-word body plus a plugin-name or filename regex on the header. `[GMODE]` checks a ≥100-word body plus the `[GMODE] <reason>` format. *[ref: per-prefix-shape-gates | .claude/plugins/job_core/hooks/question-capture.sh:85-203 + .claude/plugins/plugin_integrity/hooks/lock-manager.sh:184-200,400-420 + .claude/plugins/phasic_system/hooks/gmode-gate.sh:69-83 + .claude/context/job-system.md "Question shape" | `[JOB-COMPLETE]` (capture.sh:85-131): 4 slots — name-in-text, Review option, Approve completion option, ≥100-word body. The glossary's per-prefix shape catalog lists `[JOB-APPROVE-CREATION]` (Proposed Objective / Plugins to Touch / Why Now / Risk Surface), `[GMODE]` (Why Maintenance Lane / What I'll Touch / Why Not a Normal Job / Exit Criteria), and `[PLUGIN-LOCK]` (Plugin / Specific Changes / Why / Tests Will Run). `[PLUGIN-LOCK]`/`[TEST-LOCK]` (lock-manager.sh): ≥100-word body + name regex today. `[GMODE]` (gmode-gate.sh): ≥100-word body + format. Plan approval is no longer a separate prefixed ceremony — the completion decision now rides entirely on `[JOB-COMPLETE]` at the final cycle's CONDENSE.]*

The shape IS the cognition — filling each slot is structured reasoning the gate forces before the user is asked. The seed agent cannot ask `[JOB-COMPLETE]` without first writing a substantial review block (the current prototype floors it at a hundred words); it cannot ask `[JOB-APPROVE-CREATION]` without naming the plugins the proposed job would touch and its risk surface; it cannot ask `[GMODE]` without justifying the maintenance the mode opens. The slot-set IS the agent's compelled think-through. Each handler is a tiny stencil; what makes it work is that the agent has to fill it before the question can land. *[ref: slot-set-is-compelled-thinking | .claude/plugins/job_core/hooks/question-capture.sh:125-131 + .claude/plugins/plugin_integrity/hooks/lock-manager.sh:188-194 | The word-floor lives in both handlers as the same shape: `q_word_count=$(echo "$QUESTION" | wc -w); if [[ "$q_word_count" -lt "${WORD_MIN:-100}" ]]; then ... exit 2; fi`. Comment in lock-manager: "Forces seed agent to think ahead about WHY this lock is needed, HOW it will edit, and what the plugin looks like BEFORE vs AFTER." The gate runs BEFORE format-regex so a short body never hides behind a "bad format" voice — the cognition is compelled before the question can land.]*

Coverage is uneven by design. The ceremonies whose answer drives state changes — job completion, plugin-touch authorization, plugin unlock — carry the heaviest slot enforcement. The lighter prefixes (`[WAITING]` among them) check format and admit. Two surfaces (`[REPORT-TO-UPSTREAM]`, `[JOB-APPROVE-CREATION]`) capture the user's answer after it lands — admission shape lives on the answer side, not the question side. Whether to extend section-header shape (`WHY:` / `HOW:` / `BEFORE:` / `AFTER:` parsed from the body) to every prefix is the next maturation question — measure where word-floor falls short before hardening the registry into stencils. *[ref: coverage-uneven-by-design | .claude/plugins/lib/question_shape/prefix-shapes.conf (per-prefix entries) + .claude/plugins/plugin_integrity/hooks/upstream-reporter-hook.sh + .claude/plugins/job_core/hooks/question-capture-hook.sh "JOB-APPROVE-CREATION Handler" | Body-shape enforcement is uneven by entry in `prefix-shapes.conf`, whose header states prefixes "without an entry are NOT shape-enforced (backward-compatible opt-in)." The state-change ceremonies carry the heaviest slot sets: `[PLUGIN-LOCK]`/`[TEST-LOCK]` (WHY,HOW,BEFORE,AFTER:20), `[JOB-COMPLETE]` (5 sections incl. an empty-required Outstanding Items), `[JOB-APPROVE-CREATION]` (PROPOSED NAME,OBJECTIVE,PLUGINS TOUCHED,RISK:20). `[WAITING]` is lighter (QUESTION,WHY NOT LOW VALUE:15) and is admitted by a format-check in gmode-gate.sh's WAITING guard. Two surfaces capture the user's answer AFTER it lands — `upstream-reporter-hook.sh` (a post-capture hook for `[REPORT-TO-UPSTREAM]`) and `question-capture-hook.sh`'s JOB-APPROVE-CREATION handler (PostToolUse, fires after the user answers). The opt-in design — whether to extend section-shape to every prefix — is the maturation question the conf header frames.]*

Shape lives on both sides — the question body and the option labels — and the design that closes the coverage gap is to give every prefix a body section-set that names what the question is FOR, plus an option-label set that names what the user is choosing between. `[WAITING]` is the test case for the body side. Today it admits any non-empty reason after the prefix; the next iteration would carry two required body sections — the actual question, and a justification block explaining why this needs user input and isn't a decision the seed agent should make alone. The shape block becomes the seed's own self-check: if it can't write the justification, it doesn't ask the question. The gate stops being a registry-membership filter and starts being a low-value-question filter — the agent reasons about whether the ask is worth the user's attention BEFORE making it. *[ref: waiting-current-vs-future-shape | .claude/plugins/question_discipline/hooks/question-discipline-gate.sh PREFIX_REGISTRY array + .claude/plugins/phasic_system/hooks/gmode-gate.sh:54-63 | `[WAITING]` is admitted by registry membership (one literal in the PREFIX_REGISTRY array). Its only current shape enforcement is the format gate in gmode-gate.sh:54-63: `if echo "$QUESTION" | grep -q '^\[WAITING\]'; then reason=$(... sed ...); [[ -z "$reason" ]] && BLOCKED; exit 0`. Non-empty reason after the prefix admits — no body word-floor, no required sections, no justification block. The "two required body sections" shape described above is the next-iteration design, not today's code.]*

The same discipline generalizes to every prefix; each body shape names what the question is about, which forces the seed to decide what it's about to do BEFORE handing the choice to the user. The coverage gap closes when every prefix carries a shape rich enough that filling it is the thinking — and the registry stops being a membership filter, becomes a slot-cognition filter that turns each ask into a deliberate, structured move.

There is one more turn of this idea, and it belongs to the job rather than the prefix. The per-prefix catalog is fixed — every job's `[JOB-COMPLETE]` answers the same standard sections. But a long job often has its own evidence dimensions the standard sections don't capture. So beyond the catalog, a single job can carry its own **Extended shape**: a handful of extra `## ` sections with flexible, job-specific titles — `## Plugin Surfaces Touched This Run`, `## Visual-Asset Implications` — that this one job declares are part of what "done" means for it. The job names those sections early, while it is first loading context, and from then on they ride alongside the standard ones. When the final cycle's `[JOB-COMPLETE]` question fires, the completion check validates the standard sections *and* the job's own declared sections — all present, all filled — before the question can reach the user. *[ref: extended-shape-job-specific-addition | .claude/context/job-system.md "Extended shape" + .claude/context/job-system.md "Question shape" | The glossary defines Extended shape as "the job-specific addition to the standard Question shape catalog — N flexible-titled `## ` sections defined during cycle 1 OBSERVE objective expansion when the job's evidence dimensions go beyond what the standard catalog covers," with examples `## B6 Series State Snapshot` / `## Plugin Surfaces Touched This Run` / `## Visual-Asset Implications`. It is inherited by `[JOB-COMPLETE]` at cycle N — the question gate validates BOTH the standard sections AND the job's declared extended-shape sections, all present + non-empty — and stored on the job object as `objective_extended_shape`. The mechanism is "intentionally simple — no complex DSL, no schema validation beyond 'section title exists + has content.'"]*

The shape of the idea is clean, and the prototype ships it: the job field holds the declared section titles, a cycle-1 setter lets a job declare them, the cycle-1 OBSERVE advance checks the declared sections exist in the expanded objective, and the inheritance check folds them into the completion gate. Each job carries its own definition of finished evidence without ever touching the shared per-prefix catalog. The catalog stays the same for everyone; the job adds what only it cares about. *[ref: extended-shape-build-state | .claude/context/job-system.md "Extended shape" + .claude/plugins/job_core/scripts/job.sh extended-shape setter + .claude/plugins/question_discipline/hooks/question-discipline-gate.sh job-aware check | The built mechanism: `objective_extended_shape` is born `[]` on every job and declared via `job.sh update extended-shape '<json-array-of-titles>'` (focused-job-only — the update command takes no id argument); the cycle-1 `observe-commit.sh --force` gate requires each declared title present + non-empty in the objective; the `[JOB-COMPLETE]` question gate runs the job-aware inheritance check against the focused job's declared titles. The per-prefix catalog itself is unchanged by the addition.]*

## What would break without it

Without `question_discipline`, every `AskUserQuestion` is fair game — the agent's asking surface bloats with low-value confirmations, and the structured ceremonies (locks, approvals, completion claims) lose the prefix scaffolding the rest of the always-on layer uses to dispatch on. *[ref: without-question-discipline-asking | .claude/plugins/question_discipline/CLAUDE.md Objective section | Objective: "Block any `AskUserQuestion` whose every question's text does not begin with a registered prefix. Forces every interactive question through the established prefix system... instead of casual proceed/pause/continue prompts." Without this gate every ask passes unchecked.]*

## What you would customize

`question_discipline` is the smallest plugin in the always-on layer but the most *catalytic* — every new ceremony in the rest of your seed needs an entry here. *[ref: question-discipline-smallest-catalytic | .claude/plugins/question_discipline/ + .claude/CLAUDE.md Components section | The plugin ships one gate hook (`question-discipline-gate.sh`) and one per-prefix array — 36 tests across one suite, the smallest of all always-on plugins per the test-count snapshot in `.claude/CLAUDE.md` (compare `plugin_integrity` 604 / `phase_execute` 376 / `brain_guard` 162 / `interaction_summary` 83). It carries no scripts, no per-prefix Post handlers of its own, no data.json state — only the registry array. "Catalytic" is structural: every plugin that wants to ask the user something must register a prefix here first, then wire its Post-side handler in its own plugin tree. Every new ceremony in the rest of the seed routes through this one array.]*

You would *extend the registry*. The current registry is this prototype's catalog of structured-asking situations. Your seed will need its own — a research seed may want `[CITATION-CHECK]` for verifying a quoted source; a consulting seed may want `[CLIENT-APPROVAL]` for the equivalent of a client sign-off on a deliverable. Each new entry pairs with: a per-prefix handler in the plugin that owns the ceremony, a voice for failure messages, the slot-set the handler demands (see *How shape compels cognition* above — word-floor, named-token requirements, option-label requirements, phase or state matches), and a clear semantic — what *kind* of question this prefix marks. Names are cheap; the discipline is in keeping each prefix narrow and orthogonal — and in making the slot-set rich enough that filling it IS structured thinking. *[ref: current-prefix-registry-entries | .claude/plugins/question_discipline/hooks/question-discipline-gate.sh PREFIX_REGISTRY array + .claude/context/job-system.md "Question shape" | The canonical registry centers on `[PLUGIN-LOCK]`, `[TEST-LOCK]`, `[GMODE]`, `[JOB-COMPLETE]`, `[WAITING]`, `[REPORT-TO-UPSTREAM]`, and `[JOB-APPROVE-CREATION]`. Order doesn't matter for matching — sorted by frequency for readability. Adding a new prefix requires three coordinated edits: append the literal to the array, add an accept test in `tests/test-question-discipline-gate.sh`, and wire a per-prefix Post handler in the owning plugin (e.g., gmode-gate.sh, lock-manager.sh, question-capture.sh).]*

You would tune the *batch-cascade strictness*. The current shape rejects the entire batch on any unprefixed question. A more lenient shape might accept partial batches (process the prefixed questions, drop the unprefixed). A stricter shape might require *the same* prefix across all questions in a batch (preventing prefix mixing). The cascade is the discipline; the exact rule is yours. *[ref: batch-cascade-current-rule | .claude/plugins/question_discipline/hooks/question-discipline-gate.sh:107-135 | Current strictness: `for ((i = 0; i < QCOUNT; i++))` walks every question; on any mismatch, `all_ok=0`; after the loop, `if [[ "$all_ok" -eq 0 ]]` rejects the entire batch with a bulleted list of bad questions. Lenient variant = drop the `all_ok` aggregation, exit 2 only on the prefixed ones. Same-prefix variant = capture the first matched prefix, require equality on subsequent iterations. The loop shape is the seam where new strictness rules attach.]*

You would re-think the *subagent bypass*. The current prototype lets dispatched subagents bypass the gate because they should not become a second user-facing question surface; uncertainty should route back to the parent agent, which asks through the prefix system. Your architecture may prefer to extend the same discipline downward — every subagent question also carries a prefix, every ceremony reaches recursively. The trade-off: more discipline but more ceremony in subagent dispatch. *[ref: subagent-bypass-customization-point | .claude/plugins/question_discipline/hooks/question-discipline-gate.sh:47 | The bypass is one line, top of the hook, before any registry read: `[[ "${CLAUDE_SUBAGENT:-}" == "true" ]] && exit 0`. Removing that line is the simplest tightening — every subagent ask then walks the registry like the main session's. Alternatively, gate the bypass on a subagent allowlist or restrict it to read-only subagent types. The hook's bypass placement (before registry walk, before stdin parsing) means removal cost is one git-diff line + the test-suite changes that re-enable subagent question testing.]*

You would add the `[WAITING]` *escape valve or remove it*. The current prototype keeps `[WAITING]` as a deliberate generic for situations that don't fit a structured ceremony. Your seed may decide that the escape valve is a leak — and every question must fit one of the named ceremonies. Tightening the registry tightens the architecture; loosening it eases day-to-day work. Both are defensible. *[ref: waiting-escape-valve-registry-entry | .claude/plugins/question_discipline/hooks/question-discipline-gate.sh:74 | `[WAITING]` is one literal entry in the PREFIX_REGISTRY array: `"[WAITING]"`. Removal is a one-line array edit — delete the entry and any unprefixed conversational ask now fails the registry walk. Tightening = leave the registry intact and add a body word-floor + section-set in the Pre-side handler (mirrors the next-iteration shape described in the body above).]*

What you would **not** do is leave the gate off. Without it, the asking surface bloats with casual "proceed/pause/continue" confirmations, and the rest of the always-on layer's prefix-dispatched ceremonies (PLUGIN-LOCK, JOB-COMPLETE, JOB-APPROVE-CREATION) lose the scaffolding they hang on.
